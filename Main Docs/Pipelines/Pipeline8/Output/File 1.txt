Anschließend meldete sie sich freiwillig zum Arbeitsdienst, um von zu Hause weg zu kommen. Sie kam in ein Lager in Mülheim an der Möhne und fühlte sich dort sehr wohl. Bügeln und Kochen, und half in kinderreichen Haushalten und bei Bauern aus. Nach dem Arbeitsdienst arbeitete sie in einem Büro in Dortmund und wurde später in ein anderes Lager ausgelagert. English:

The interviewee was born in 1925 in Hemer in the Sauerland. She was the first grandchild and was very spoiled because she spent a lot of time with her grandmother. She suffered from migraines and was therefore not allowed to go to high school. After graduating from secondary school in 1939, she had to complete a compulsory year, which she spent in a country year camp. There she learned many skills, such as sewing and darning, which she had not previously known. After the country year, she did an office apprenticeship and worked in an office for two years. She then volunteered for labor service to get away from home. She was sent to a camp in Mülheim an der Möhne and felt very comfortable there. The daily routine in the camp was strictly regulated, with early morning exercise, work and political training. The interviewee learned many new skills, such as ironing and cooking, and helped out in households with many children and with farmers. After the labor service, she worked in an office in Dortmund and was later transferred to another camp. In 1944 she married a man who had been severely disabled in the war and moved with him to Ilmenau. After the war they returned to Gelsenkirchen-Buer and the interviewee had to look after her children alone after her husband died in 1949. She received a pension of 120 marks and had to live very frugally. She opened a lending library and later a drinking hall, which she ran for seven years. She then worked at Giradet and later in an office job, which she still does today. She is very happy with her work and finds that she learned a lot from the year in the country and the labor service, such as how to manage her time and assert herself. She believes that these experiences helped her to change her life.
